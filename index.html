Using Machine Learning to Predict Wildfires in CA

Comparing different models

Background

Many factors might contribute to the spread of wildfire, including climate and vegetation. California has a Mediterranean climate with warm winters, and cool and dry summers. Because of this, California is very prone to wildfires.
Machine Learning (ML) is a rapidly growing field of computer science, and new applications for it are being discovered every day. Many different methods can be used to train a ML model to predict wildfire spread, and some may be better suited to the task than others. Research done on other Mediterranean climates has found that some models like multiple linear regressions (MLR) and multilayer perceptrons (MLP) provide more accurate predictions than others


Rationale

California is known for its frequent wildfires. Because California is highly populated, wildfires there cause a lot of damage. In a year, wildfires can kill dozens, misplace thousands, and cause tens of billions of dollars in property damage. Being able to predict these fires could help mitigate this tremendously. In previous studies, Multiple Linear Regressions (MLR) and Multilayer Perceptrons (MLP) have been shown to be accurate in predicting wildfire spread. Additionally, weather, specifically temperature, plays a big role in the starting and spreading of wildfires. There is lots of data available on historical wildfires and historical temperature conditions in California, meaning that a machine learning model could be trained to predict wildfires based on temperature data.


Research practicum

In this experiment, historical fire and temperature data were used to train both an MLR and an MLP to predict wildfires in California.
